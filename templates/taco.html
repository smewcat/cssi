Tacos

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup peanut oil
  • 12 (6-inch) yellow corn tortillas
  • Kosher, for seasoning, plus 1 teaspoon for beef
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 16 ounces ground sirloin
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2/3 cup low-sodium beef broth
  • 6 ounces panela cheese, crumbled
  • 12 pickled jalapeno slices
  • 1 cup shredded iceberg lettuce (optional)
  • 1 large tomato, seeded and chopped (optional)
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro leaves (optional)


Recipe

  1. Heat the oven to 250 degrees F.
  2. Heat peanut oil until it reaches 350 degrees (about 5 minutes), and maintain the temperature.
  3. Create a taco-shaped holder using aluminium foil, and shape 1 tortilla around it to form a taco shape. Put the bottom of the tortilla into the hot oil and fry for 20 seconds.
  4. Lay 1 side of the tortilla down in the hot oil and fry for 30 seconds.
  5. Flip the tortilla over and fry for an additional 30 seconds. Remove the taco and cool for 30 seconds before removing the mold.
  6. Sprinkle the hot tortillas with kosher salt. Repeat frying procedure with the remaining tortillas.
  7. Drain all but 2 tablespoons of peanut oil from the skillet and return to medium heat. Once the oil shimmers, add the onion and cook until softened and lightly browned around the edges, (about 3 to 4 minutes). Add the ground meat, 1 teaspoon salt, and garlic. Cook until browned (about 3 to 4 minutes), stirring occasionally to break up the meat. Add beef broth. Bring to a simmer and cook, uncovered, until sauce is slightly thickened, (2 to 3 minutes).
  8. Assemble each taco with meat mixture, panela, jalapeno slices, lettuce, tomatoes, and cilantro.
